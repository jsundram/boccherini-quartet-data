Born: 19 February 1743, Lucca, Republic of Lucca

Vienna: 1757 - 1764

* opus 2 written here

Italy: 1764-1767, quartetto toscano 1766

Paris: 1767-1768  

* opus 2 published here in 1767

Spain (1768-1805)

* 1768 - 1776 Madrid
  * 1769 op 8 string quartets dedicated to Don Luis de Bourbon
  * 1770 op 9 "alli signori diletanti di madrid" (to the gentlement of madrid)
  * family quartet "Father Font + 3 sons", joined to make string quintets, wrote the first 12 here.
* 1776 Arenas de San Pedro (100km away, isolated) - boccherni pissed of Charles IV, and Don Luis married a commoner so had to leave.
  * 1780 Opus 32 string quartets, also *la musica notturna della strade di madrid* for don luis to remind him of madrid while in exile. 
  * 1781, Haydn sends Boccherini two letters, never received?
    * "I  enclose  Herr  Boccherini's  letter:^  please  present  my  respectful 
      compliments  to  him.  No  one  here  can  tell  me  where  this  place 
      Arenas  is.  It  cannot  be  far  from  Madrid,  however;  please  let  me 
      know  about  this,  for  I  want  to  write  Herr  Boccherini  myself. 
    * I  send  you  herewith  the  enclosed^  letter,  and  only  regret  that  I 
      cannot  at  present  write  to  Herr  Boccherini  in  my  own  hand,  but 
      when  occasion  offers,  please  present  my  devoted  respects  to  him. 
    * Boccherini sends Haydn a letter (through Artaria): "if any of those gentlemen (as is likely) should know Mr. Giuseppe Haydn [sic], to whom I have written and who is esteemed by me and by everyone in the highest degree, they will offer him my respects, telling him that I am one of his most ardent admirers and devotees, both of his genius and of his musical compositions, which here are accorded all that esteem which, in strict justice, they deserve"
  * 1783 - Frederick future king of prussia received boccherinis opus 33 quartets and says "Nothing could give me so much pleasure, Signor Boccherini, as receiving your compositions, written in your own hand, and at the moment when I can perform them on your instrument, the cello. They have given me complete satisfaction, and if I enjoy this pleasure today, I want to believe that your taste for composition will not stop there; I dare to hope that I will see some more of your works, which, in that case, will give me great pleasure. In the meantime, please accept, Signor Boccherini, this gold box as a memento of me and as a token of the esteem I have for your talents in an art that I love"
  * 1784, Boccherini meets Goya and may be painted into the family portrait
  * 1785, wife dies and so dues don luis
* 1785-1799 Madrid, new patron, future king of prussia
  * 1787 remarries (daughter of a cellist)
  * writes about 12 pieces a year for these 12 years, 104 survive. However, his contract didn't allow him to publish them, so they were only published posthumously 
  * 1797 Frederick Wilhelm II dies.
  * Boccherini negotiates with Pleyel to publish opus numbers 44-54, then separately 21, 25, 27-43, and then 55-56 
* 1799 - 1801 Lucien Bonaparte (ambasador to Madrid), Napoleon's younger brother.

* 1801 - no patron daughters die.
* 1803 - final pulications of works during boccherini's lifetime (quartets opus 58)
* 1804 - opus 64 quartets were supposed to be a set of 6, only one was complete, only 1 movement of the second quartet exists.



Died: 28 May 1805, Madrid, Kingdom of Spain
